# Contributing

Thank you for helping keep this resource guide up to date.

## Scope

This repository collects resources for economists who want to contribute directly to the development of AI systems. The focus is on applying microeconomic theory, game theory, econometrics, industrial organization, and behavioral economics to AI post-training, alignment, safety, mechanism design for AI, and multi-agent systems.

**In scope:**
- Fellowships, summer schools, and programs that place economists in AI research or safety roles
- Research communities and venues where economists publish on AI system design
- Learning resources for the technical stack economists need (ML, RL, deep learning, PyTorch)
- Papers that connect microeconomic frameworks to AI research problems

**Out of scope:**
- AI's impact on labor markets, productivity, or economic growth
- General machine learning resources with no connection to economics
- AI policy and regulation unless directly tied to mechanism design or system development
- Resources focused on using AI as a tool for economic research (e.g., ML for causal inference)

## How to contribute

- **Dead link?** Open an issue with the broken URL and, if you know it, the updated one.
- **New resource?** Open a pull request adding it to the relevant table in `README.md`. Please include the name, a one-sentence description, and a working link.
- **Corrections or suggestions?** Open an issue describing the change.

## Guidelines

- Keep descriptions concise (one to two sentences).
- Verify that links are working before submitting.
- For fellowships and programs, note if they are recurring (annual, rolling) or one-time.
